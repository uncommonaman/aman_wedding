Reception Of Barat + Anand Karaj

Sunday July 2, 10:30am

Gurudwara Gurunanak College, Velachery, Chennai
View on Map

This is the formal part of the ceremony which always takes place in a Gurdwara. You will be expected to sit on the carpeted floor for at least 45 minutes. It comprises of the following functions:

a. Kirtan - The singing of the Holy Hymns - Gurbani.
b. Ardas – Key People will stand straight with folded hands and join in this prayer.
c. Palla - The bride's father will formally hand the end of the sash, which the bridegroom wears over his shoulder into the hands of the bride
d. Laava – The Bride and Groom will walk in tow around the SGGS four times.
e. Next up is Ardas followed by Sagaan and Kara Parshad

Muhurtham

Monday July 3, 6:00am

Sri Raghavendra Kalyana Mandapam,40 Vishwanathpuram Main Road, Kodambakka, Chennai 60024
View on Map

We welcome you to participate in the wedding ceremony -

Kalyanam beginning with Mangala Issai (traditional music) of Nathaswaram and Melam (Wind & Percussion instruments),

Kanyadhanam - to symbolize that Vigna would receive Aman as a precious gift and soulful partner through their parents.

Mangalya Dharanam (tying of the sacred thread amid chanting of mantras) and the three knots symbolising the bond between the bride and groom and the families amidst the shower of tumeric soaked rice (Akshadhai) as blessing from elders which translates to "May you live and enjoy a hundred autumns together"

Maalai Maathal (Exchange of Garlands) between bride & groom

Saptapati (Seven steps around the fire) signifying :-
-To Nourish each other
-To grow together in strength
-To share our joys and sorrows
-To preserve and grow the wealth & prosperity
-To care for the children
-To care for the parents
-To be together and remain friends forever
